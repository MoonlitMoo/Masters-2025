# Paper format

# Introduction

- General background
- Specific background relevant to paper
- Layout of paper (section description)
- cosmology / coord sys / standard assumptions

# Observations

- What was used for the observation and its specs
- Details about observation settings

# Data reduction

- Flagging and calibration process
- Primary + secondary calibrators
- Imaging processing / self calibration

# Analysis

- Split into subsections for each different part looked at
- What look at → numbers we get.
- Essentially just the methods of what was done to the data, reasoning why do it and what came out.
- Many of these flow a → b since b was prompted/inspired by a.

# Discussion

- Adds specific information not given in introduction relevant to the analysis.
- Talks about previous papers in the context of this paper.
- Brings in own results to compare / contrast / support / not support findings.
- The what do the results mean and how are they important.

# Conclusions

- Bullet pointed list of the papers important points start to finish.
- Or a non-bulleted summary in the same fashion
- 1-2 sentence per point

# Acknowledgements

- Who helped in any amount
- What resources were used in the creation of this

# Data availability

- Where the raw data is and availibility
- Where the processed data and what not is

# References

- PASA formatted references.
