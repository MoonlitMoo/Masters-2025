https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0004-637X/722/1/737 (2010)

- Models GH and MH to both arise from hadronic productions of cosmic ray ion collisions. Supposes the CRI are from supernovae.
- Finds a linear correlation between halo size and brightness.
- Traces magnetic field from the radio spectrum, fields are powered by mergers / core sloshing.
- Mention of transition between GH and MH.

---

https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2009/44/aa12751-09/aa12751-09.html (2009)

- Assumes a link of GH to turbulent reacceleration

---

https://arxiv.org/abs/1203.2994 (2012)

- Simulation of turbulence of sloshing core, causing re-acceleration of electrons.
- Uses seed low-relativistic electrons from prior AGN / hadronic.
- Sloshing causes redistribution and re-acceleration of seed electrons, with the resulting spectra looking like a minihalo.
- Turbulence bounded by cold fronts, there is an expected steepening of radio emission at high frequencies.

---

https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0004-637X/801/2/146 (2015)

- Simulation of hadronic production of highly relativistic electrons as follow on from above.
- Not precisely confined to the cold fronts, unless strong magnetic field differences.
- Spectrum is mostly consistent with a power-law, only some regions experience steepening due to rapidly fluctuating magnetic fields.
- Highlights differences of spatial + spectral in comparison to turbulence as a driver.

# Clusters

## 2A0335+096

- https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2022A%26A...659A..20I/abstract (2022)
    - Measured spectral index of minihalo 144 MHz - 1.5 GHz ($\alpha=-1.2\pm0.1$) and the lobes ($\alpha=-1.5\pm0.1$). Diffuse minihalo may be connected to AGN as source, but secondary mechanism for re-acceleration.
    - Proposed re-acceleration of the minihalo is turbulence in the core over hadronic. Still difficult to discern, not a definite call.
    - NAT tail is Mpc long, larger than cooling scale, propose gentle re-acceleration as cause.
- https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2007AJ....134...14D/abstract (2007)
    - Companion galaxy to BCG interacted with it in the past, now moving away from it.
    - Interaction may have triggered radio source, due to similarities in time scale between last interaction / age of radio source.
    - VLA radio image 3” resolution to 0.5 mJy  with NE lobe moving towards us, SW node moving away from us. Data from 1987.
- https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1995ApJ...451..125S/abstract (1995)
    - Radio image VLA (C+D) 1.515 GHz 16” tapered resolution.
    - First? observations of the radio lobes + NAT radio galaxy.
- https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/1538-4357/ab29f1 (2019)
    - 1.4 GHz image + notes on previous studies.
