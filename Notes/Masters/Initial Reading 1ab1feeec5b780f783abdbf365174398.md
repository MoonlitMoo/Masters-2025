![image.png](Initial%20Reading/image.png)

- Bremsstrahlung radiation. 
Free-free emission from electrons passing by nuclei, deviating due to attraction/repulsion. Curve causes acceleration and changed of energy emitted as a function of velocity. Able to estimate density and temperature of ionized gases.
- Thermal radiation of dust
Blackbody radiation at the Rayleigh-Jeans limit for radio frequencies. Polarised perpendicular to magnetic field. Distinguish from background sources.
- Synchrotron emission
Relativistic electrons spiraling through a magnetic field. Polarised emission and relativistic beaming effects apply.
